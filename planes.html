⊗ NAVIGATING THE PLANES OF CONSCIOUSNESS ⊗
WARNING: Understanding these planes may permanently alter your perception of reality. These are not metaphors - they are actual dimensions of consciousness.

⊗ THE FORWARD PLANE ⊗

Known across traditions as:

  • Tibetan: Nang-pa (Inner Space)
  • Hermetic: Imaginatio Vera
  • Islamic: Alam al-mithal

CHARACTERISTICS:

  • Active imagination and conscious visualization
  • Where we first learn to "see" with closed eyes
  • The realm of intentional creation
  • Laboratory of consciousness

PRACTICES:

  • Creating and holding forms
  • Working with sacred geometry
  • Deity visualization
  • Construction of inner temples

⊗ THE BACKWARD PLANE ⊗

Known across traditions as:

  • Platonic: World of Forms
  • Jungian: Collective Unconscious
  • Shamanic: Spirit World

CHARACTERISTICS:

  • Astral perception
  • Archetypal realms
  • Autonomous imagery
  • Living symbols

PHENOMENA:

  • Encounters with autonomous beings
  • Spontaneous visions
  • Mythological imagery
  • Direct transmission of knowledge

⊗ THE UPPER PLANE/CROWN ⊗

Known across traditions as:

  • Buddhist: Dharmakaya
  • Hindu: Sahasrara
  • Kabbalah: Keter

CHARACTERISTICS:

  • Causal connection
  • Energetic integration point
  • Non-dual awareness
  • Transcendent consciousness

MANIFESTATIONS:

  • Pure light experiences
  • Dissolution of subject-object duality
  • Direct knowing
  • Integration of all planes

⊗ NAVIGATION TECHNIQUES ⊗

FORWARD PLANE ACCESS:

  • Close eyes, observe phosphenes
  • Direct attention to visual space
  • Begin with simple forms
  • Progress to complex visualization

BACKWARD PLANE ACCESS:

  • Release control of imagery
  • Allow autonomous movement
  • Follow spontaneous visions
  • Engage with emerging figures

UPPER PLANE ACCESS:

  • Maintain awareness of crown center
  • Allow dissolution of imagery
  • Rest in pure consciousness
  • Integration of all experiences

⊗ COMMON PITFALLS ⊗

  • FORWARD PLANE:
    • - Forcing visualization - Expecting physical sight - Judging progress
  • BACKWARD PLANE:
    • - Controlling autonomous content - Dismissing experiences as "just imagination" - Fear of the unknown
  • UPPER PLANE:
    • - Seeking specific experiences - Attachment to phenomena - Premature integration
REMEMBER: These planes are not separate but interpenetrating dimensions of consciousness. Your experience may vary. Trust your process.
